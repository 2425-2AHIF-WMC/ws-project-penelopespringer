Marsha P. Johnson

Marsha P. Johnson: Pioneering Activist and LGBTQ+ Advocate

  • Born on August 24, 1945, in Elizabeth, New Jersey
  • Co-founder of the Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries (STAR) alongside Sylvia Rivera
  • Renowned for her activism during the Stonewall Uprising in 1969

Marsha P. Johnson was a transformative figure in the fight for LGBTQ+ rights:

  • Instrumental in advocating for the rights of marginalized communities, particularly transgender and homeless individuals
  • Worked with organizations focused on AIDS awareness and support during the epidemic
  • Her activism challenged systemic discrimination and violence against LGBTQ+ people

Audre Lorde

Audre Lorde: A Trailblazing Poet and Activist

  • Born on February 18, 1934, in New York City
  • Known for her powerful poetry and essays addressing race, gender, and sexuality
  • Co-founder of the Women’s Institute for Freedom of the Press

Audre Lorde was an influential voice in the fight for social justice:

  • Championed the rights of women of color and LGBTQ+ individuals
  • Emphasized the importance of self-care and community activism
  • Her legacy continues to inspire new generations of activists and writers

Stormé DeLarverie

Stormé DeLarverie: A Pioneering Drag King and Activist

  • Born on December 24, 1920, in New Orleans, Louisiana
  • Known as one of the first drag kings and a prominent figure in the Stonewall Uprising
  • Advocated for LGBTQ+ rights throughout her life

Stormé DeLarverie was a trailblazer in the fight for equality:

  • Often referred to as the "Rosa Parks of the Stonewall Riots"
  • Fiercely defended herself and others during the riots
  • Her legacy continues to inspire activism and pride within the LGBTQ+ community

Dr. Mary Golda Ross

Dr. Mary Golda Ross: Pioneering Aerospace Engineer

  • Born on August 9, 1908, in Park Hill, Oklahoma
  • Graduated from Northeastern State Teachers College and earned a master's degree in mathematics
  • Joined Lockheed Aircraft Corporation in 1942, becoming one of the first female engineers at NASA

Dr. Mary Golda Ross was a trailblazer in aerospace engineering during the 20th century:

  • Worked on advanced projects, including the design of spacecraft and interplanetary missions
  • Contributed to the development of systems for the Agena rocket, used for reconnaissance satellites
  • Advocated for the education of Native Americans in STEM fields throughout her life

James Baldwin

James Baldwin: Renowned Writer and Civil Rights Activist

  • Born on August 2, 1924, in New York City
  • Published his first novel, "Go Tell It on the Mountain," in 1953
  • Known for his essays and criticism on race, sexuality, and identity in America

James Baldwin was a powerful voice in the fight for civil rights:

  • His work confronted the systemic racism and inequality faced by African Americans
  • Collaborated with civil rights leaders such as Martin Luther King Jr. and Malcolm X
  • His legacy continues to influence discussions on race and social justice today

Angela Davis

Angela Davis: Activist, Scholar, and Author

  • Born on January 26, 1944, in Birmingham, Alabama
  • Gained national attention in the 1960s as a leader in the Black Panther Party
  • Known for her work on prison reform and women's rights

Angela Davis has been a prominent voice in the fight for social justice:

  • Advocated against the prison-industrial complex and for the abolition of prisons
  • Continues to speak out on issues of race, gender, and economic inequality
  • Her writings, including "Women, Race & Class," are foundational in feminist and Black studies

Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr.: Leader of the Civil Rights Movement

  • Born on January 15, 1929, in Atlanta, Georgia
  • Best known for his role in the advancement of civil rights using nonviolent civil disobedience
  • Delivered the famous "I Have a Dream" speech during the 1963 March on Washington

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. was a pivotal figure in the struggle for civil rights:

  • Led significant campaigns against racial segregation and discrimination
  • Co-founded the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC)
  • Received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964 for his efforts to combat racial injustice

Frederick Douglass

Frederick Douglass: Abolitionist and Social Reformer

  • Born in February 1818, in Talbot County, Maryland
  • Escaped slavery and became a leading voice in the abolitionist movement
  • Published his autobiography, "Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, an American Slave," in 1845

Frederick Douglass was a powerful advocate for the rights of African Americans:

  • Utilized his eloquent speeches and writings to demand equality and justice
  • Championed women's rights and suffrage
  • Advised several presidents and was a prominent figure in American politics
